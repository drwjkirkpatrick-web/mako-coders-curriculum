# Week 25 Quiz — Data Ethics — Keep Wildlife Safe — Ages 14–17

> *10 code-focused, CBE-aligned questions for Week 25: Data Ethics — Keep Wildlife Safe.*

## Questions

1. What is informed consent in data collection?
2. Explain the principle of data minimisation.
3. How can sharing GPS coordinates harm wildlife?
4. What is anonymisation?
5. Write a short data-use policy for a Kenyan marine app.
6. What is a Creative Commons license used for?
7. How should you handle community knowledge about fishing spots?
8. What is the difference between open data and private data?
9. Explain GDPR-style rights in simple terms.
10. Map data ethics to a CBE Citizenship outcome.

## Answer Key

1. People understand and agree to how their data will be used.
2. Collect only the data you actually need.
3. Poachers or tourists can find sensitive habitats.
4. Removing information that identifies a person or exact location.
5. We collect only species counts, no exact locations, and share summaries only with researchers.
6. To tell others how they may use your work.
7. Ask permission and protect sensitive details.
8. Open data can be freely shared; private data has access restrictions.
9. People have the right to know, correct, and delete their data.
10. Learners use technology responsibly and respect privacy.
