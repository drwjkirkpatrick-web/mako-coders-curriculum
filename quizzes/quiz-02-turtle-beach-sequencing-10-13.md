# Week 02 Quiz — Turtle Beach — Sequencing — Ages 10–13

> *10 code-focused, CBE-aligned questions for Week 02: Turtle Beach — Sequencing.*

## Questions

1. Put these Scratch blocks in order to make a turtle move to the sea: point in direction 90, move 10 steps, when green flag clicked.
2. What does 'sequencing' mean in coding?
3. If you swap two blocks in a sequence, what can happen?
4. Which block runs first in a Scratch script?
5. Write a 3-block sequence that makes a sprite say 'Hello' then wait 1 second then say 'Ocean'.
6. Why must a sea-turtle animation reach the water before turning around?
7. What is an algorithm?
8. How many blocks are in this sequence: event + move + turn + say?
9. In pseudocode, write three steps to draw a square.
10. What should you do after adding one new block to a script?

## Answer Key

1. 1. When green flag clicked; 2. point in direction 90; 3. move 10 steps.
2. Putting instructions in the correct order.
3. The sprite may move or act in the wrong way.
4. The block directly under the event hat (e.g., when green flag clicked).
5. When green flag clicked → say Hello for 2 secs → wait 1 secs → say Ocean for 2 secs.
6. Because order matters in a sequence.
7. A step-by-step set of instructions.
8. Four blocks.
9. 1. Move forward; 2. Turn 90 degrees; 3. Repeat four times.
10. Run the program and check the result.
